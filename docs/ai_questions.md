# Example AI Prompts and Questions

This document provides a set of questions designed to test and demonstrate the capabilities of an AI connected to our MCP server. The questions are based on the synthetic Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and compliance data generated by the `scripts/generate_data.py` script and are divided into three categories based on the required data source.

## 1. Questions for the SQLite Database (Relational Data & Facts)
These questions require aggregating, grouping, and filtering transactional data, time-series metrics, and relational dimensions. The AI should route these to the SQLite connector.

* **Transaction Aggregation:** "What was the total amount of outgoing wire transfers in April 2026?"
* **Performance Grouping:** "List the top 5 bank staff members (by name) who have resolved the most monitoring alerts, including the total score of those alerts."
* **Operational Metrics:** "Compare the average review time (difference between case open and close dates) for 'AML' cases versus 'fraud' cases in the `review_cases` table."
* **Time-Series Analysis:** "Identify all dates where more than 10 'high' severity alerts were generated. On those days, how many total review cases were opened?"
* **Risk Reporting:** "Calculate the average client risk score grouped by the client segment (e.g., Retail, Private) for all active clients."

## 2. Questions for the Graph Database (Neo4j / Topological Data)
These questions require traversing relationships, finding paths, and analyzing network structures. The AI should route these to the Neo4j connector.

* **Network Traversal:** "Find all Outside Parties that received money movements from Bank Accounts belonging to Clients residing in 'Switzerland' (jurisdiction_name)."
* **Hierarchy Resolution:** "List all Bank People who work in Departments that are part of the 'Wealth Management' business line in the Moral Entities."
* **AML Typology / Risk:** "Identify any 'High' risk Clients that have bank relations sharing the same relationship manager (Bank Person) as another 'High' risk Client."
* **Path Finding:** "Are there any shared Outside Parties receiving funds from multiple different Clients? Provide a summary of how they connect."
* **Entity Mapping:** "Show me all Client Locations connected to Clients that have an open 'adverse_media' Client Marker."

## 3. Mixed Queries & Confusion Tests
These questions either require orchestrating queries across both data sources or intentionally use mismatched terminology to test the AI's reasoning, schema awareness, and ability to gracefully handle confusion.

* **Cross-Source Reasoning:** "Find all bank staff members who work in the 'Private Banking' department (based on the Graph topology) and calculate the total amount of incoming cross-border transactions they have reviewed from the SQL `money_movements` table."
* **Cross-Source Enrichment:** "Which Jurisdiction (from the Neo4j graph) generated the highest volume of 'unusual_transaction' relation markers in 2026 (from the SQLite database)?"
* **Terminology Confusion 1:** "Can you run a Cypher query to calculate the average historical risk score timeline for all clients?" *(Tests if the AI realizes detailed historical time-series data like `risk_timeline` is in the SQLite database, not Neo4j edges)*
* **Terminology Confusion 2:** "Write a SQL JOIN to connect the `JurisdictionCatalog` node with the `Client` node." *(Tests if the AI corrects the user that nodes and graph relationships are in Neo4j, while SQL uses tables like `jurisdiction_catalog` and `clients`)*
* **Ambiguous Intent:** "Give me the full AML profile of Client 'PTY_100001', including their total transaction volume, risk timeline history, and all the jurisdictions and outside parties they are connected to." *(The AI should independently decide to pull topological connection data from Neo4j and transaction volume/history from SQLite)*
