# Example AI Prompts and Questions

This document provides a set of questions designed to test and demonstrate the capabilities of an AI connected to our MCP server. The questions are based on the synthetic data generated by the `scripts/generate_data.py` script and are divided into three categories based on the required data source.

## 1. Questions for the SQLite Database (Relational Data & Facts)
These questions require aggregating, grouping, and filtering transactional data, time-series metrics, and relational dimensions. The AI should route these to the SQLite connector.

* **Financial Aggregation:** "What was the total revenue, total cost, and overall PnL in USD for the 'baseline' scenario during Q1 2025?"
* **Performance Grouping:** "List the top 5 traders who generated the highest PnL across all fact tables. Include the desk they belong to and their seniority."
* **Operational Metrics:** "Compare the average execution latency (in ms) and slippage (in bps) for trades handled via the 'algo' channel versus the 'voice' channel."
* **Time-Series Analysis:** "Identify all trade dates where the market stress level was categorized as 'stressed'. On those days, what was the total number of trade fails (`fail_flag = 1`)?"
* **Risk Reporting:** "Calculate the total notional traded for products in the 'Tier-3' liquidity tier, grouped by customer credit bucket."

## 2. Questions for the Graph Database (Neo4j / Topological Data)
These questions require traversing relationships, finding paths, and analyzing network structures. The AI should route these to the Neo4j connector.

* **Network Traversal:** "Find all Counterparties that have an active relationship with Customers based in 'Switzerland' (`CH`)."
* **Hierarchy Resolution:** "List all Traders who work at Desks located in countries that belong to the 'EMEA' region."
* **Systemic Risk:** "Identify any 'systemic' Counterparties (where `systemic_flag` is true/1) that hold relationships of type 'Prime' with more than 5 different Customers."
* **Path Finding:** "Are there any shared relationships between customers in 'Japan' and counterparties in the 'United States'? Provide a summary of how they connect."
* **Entity Mapping:** "Show me the distribution of product risk classes within the 'Rates' Product Family."

## 3. Mixed Queries & Confusion Tests
These questions either require orchestrating queries across both data sources or intentionally use mismatched terminology to test the AI's reasoning, schema awareness, and ability to gracefully handle confusion.

* **Cross-Source Reasoning:** "Find traders who work in the 'APAC' region (based on the Graph topology) and calculate their average trade execution latency and total PnL from the SQL fact tables."
* **Cross-Source Enrichment:** "Which systemic counterparty (from the Neo4j graph) generated the highest total notional traded volume in Q2 2025 (from the SQLite database)?"
* **Terminology Confusion 1:** "Can you calculate the total PnL and revenue generated by the edges in the Neo4j database?" *(Tests if the AI realizes PnL is in the SQLite fact tables, not Neo4j edges)*
* **Terminology Confusion 2:** "Run a Cypher query to select all rows from the `market_daily_signal` table and join it with the `dim_calendar` node." *(Tests if the AI corrects the user that `market_daily_signal` is a SQL table, not a Neo4j node)*
* **Ambiguous Intent:** "Give me the full profile of Customer 'Customer_0042', including their trading performance, credit bucket, and all the counterparties they are connected to." *(The AI should independently decide to pull relationship data from Neo4j and performance data from SQLite)*
